Energy-Momentum Relationship

Chapter: Special Theory of Relativity

The energy-momentum relationship is a foundational result from Einstein's Special Theory of Relativity. It links the total energy of a particle to its momentum and rest mass.

$$ E^2 = (pc)^2 + (m_0c^2)^2 $$

Where:

  • E is the total energy
  • p is the relativistic momentum
  • m0 is the rest mass
  • c is the speed of light

Why This Equation Matters

This equation generalizes the famous \( E = mc^2 \) for moving objects. It shows how energy and momentum are deeply intertwined, especially at high speeds.

Real World Examples

  • Particle Accelerators: High-speed particles like electrons and protons follow this relationship. It's used to design accelerators like the LHC.
  • Cosmic Rays: Extremely energetic particles from space obey this equation.
  • Photon Energy: For photons (massless particles), \( E = pc \).

Derivation Insight

From Newtonian mechanics, we know \( E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \). But in relativity, we use:

$$ E = \gamma m_0 c^2, \quad p = \gamma m_0 v $$

Combining and eliminating \( \gamma \), we arrive at the energy-momentum relationship.

Interactive Quiz

Q: What is the energy of a photon with momentum \( 3.3 \times 10^{-27} \) kg·m/s?

Using \( E = pc \),
\( E = 3.3 \times 10^{-27} \times 3 \times 10^8 = 9.9 \times 10^{-19} \) J

Practical Applications

  • Nuclear Power: Conversion between mass and energy relies on \( E = mc^2 \).
  • GPS Systems: Account for relativistic corrections in satellite clocks.
  • Medical Imaging: PET scans detect annihilation photons calculated using energy-momentum relationships.

Fun Fact

Did you know? When you eat food, a tiny amount of its mass is converted into energy to power your cells—Einstein’s equation at work!

More Interactive Problems

Q: If a particle has rest mass \( m_0 = 1 \) kg and moves at 0.8c, what is its total energy?

\( \gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}} = 1.666 \)
\( E = \gamma m_0 c^2 = 1.666 \times 1 \times (3 \times 10^8)^2 = 1.5 \times 10^{17} \) J
