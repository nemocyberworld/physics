Physics Topic: Vector Triple Product

1. Definition

The vector triple product is an operation involving three vectors in 3D space. The expression for the vector triple product is:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) $$

This means that we take the cross product of vector \(\vec{A}\) with the cross product of vectors \(\vec{B}\) and \(\vec{C}\).

2. Vector Triple Product Identity

The vector triple product identity is a formula used to simplify expressions involving the vector triple product:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) = (\vec{A} \cdot \vec{C})\vec{B} - (\vec{A} \cdot \vec{B})\vec{C} $$

This identity is helpful because it allows us to rewrite the vector triple product as a combination of dot products and simpler cross products.

3. Geometric Interpretation

In geometrical terms, the cross product \(\vec{B} \times \vec{C}\) results in a vector that is perpendicular to both \(\vec{B}\) and \(\vec{C}\). When we cross this result with \(\vec{A}\), the result is a vector that lies in the plane formed by \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\), and its magnitude is proportional to the area of the parallelogram formed by these vectors.

4. Example 1: Simplifying a Vector Triple Product

Given the following vectors:

$$ \vec{A} = 2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} + \hat{k} $$
$$ \vec{B} = \hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 4\hat{k} $$
$$ \vec{C} = 3\hat{i} + \hat{j} - 5\hat{k} $$

Let's compute \(\vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C})\) using the identity.

First, calculate the dot products:

$$ \vec{A} \cdot \vec{C} = (2)(3) + (3)(1) + (1)(-5) = 4 $$
$$ \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = (2)(1) + (3)(-2) + (1)(4) = 0 $$

Now, using the identity:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) = (4)\vec{B} - (0)\vec{C} = 4\vec{B} $$

Multiply vector \(\vec{B}\) by 4:

$$ 4\vec{B} = 4(\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}) = 4\hat{i} - 8\hat{j} + 16\hat{k} $$

Thus, the simplified result is:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) = 4\hat{i} - 8\hat{j} + 16\hat{k} $$

$$ \vec{A} = 3\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} $$
$$ \vec{B} = \hat{i} + \hat{j} $$
$$ \vec{C} = 2\hat{i} - \hat{j} $$

We will compute the cross product \(\vec{B} \times \vec{C}\) and then find the vector triple product \(\vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C})\).

First, compute the cross product \(\vec{B} \times \vec{C}\):

$$ \vec{B} \times \vec{C} = \left| \begin{matrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 2 & -1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right| = -3\hat{k} $$

Now, compute \(\vec{A} \times (-3\hat{k})\):

$$ \vec{A} \times (-3\hat{k}) = (-3)(3\hat{i} + 2\hat{j}) \times \hat{k} = 6\hat{i} - 9\hat{j} $$

Thus, the vector triple product is:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) = 6\hat{i} - 9\hat{j} $$

6. Application: Simplifying Complex Expressions

We can apply the vector triple product identity to simplify complex vector expressions. For instance, consider:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times (\vec{C} \times \vec{D})) $$

Using the identity:

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times (\vec{C} \times \vec{D})) = (\vec{A} \cdot \vec{D})\vec{B} - (\vec{A} \cdot \vec{B})\vec{C} \times \vec{D} $$

This simplification eliminates the need to compute the triple cross product directly.
