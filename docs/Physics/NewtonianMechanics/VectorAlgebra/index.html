Vector Algebra Course Summary

1. Fundamentals of Vectors

Vector: Quantity with both magnitude and direction.

Zero Vector: \( \vec{0} \) has zero magnitude and no direction.

Unit Vector: \( \hat{u} = \vec{A} / |\vec{A}| \)

Collinear Vectors: Lie along the same or parallel lines.

Coplanar Vectors: Lie in the same plane.

2. Vector Addition and Subtraction

$$ \vec{A} + \vec{B} = \vec{B} + \vec{A} $$
$$ (\vec{A} + \vec{B}) + \vec{C} = \vec{A} + (\vec{B} + \vec{C}) $$
$$ \vec{A} + \vec{0} = \vec{A} $$
$$ \vec{A} + (-\vec{A}) = \vec{0} $$
$$ \vec{A} - \vec{B} = \vec{A} + (-\vec{B}) $$

3. Scalar (Dot) Product

$$ \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = |\vec{A}||\vec{B}|\cos\theta $$
$$ \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} $$
$$ \vec{A} \cdot (\vec{B} + \vec{C}) = \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} + \vec{A} \cdot \vec{C} $$

Orthogonal vectors: \( \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = 0 \)

4. Vector (Cross) Product

$$ \vec{A} \times \vec{B} = |\vec{A}||\vec{B}|\sin\theta \hat{n} $$
$$ \vec{A} \times \vec{B} = -\vec{B} \times \vec{A} $$
$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} + \vec{C}) = \vec{A} \times \vec{B} + \vec{A} \times \vec{C} $$
$$ \vec{A} \times \vec{A} = \vec{0} $$

5. Scalar Triple Product

$$ \vec{A} \cdot (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) $$

Equals volume of parallelepiped. Zero if coplanar.

6. Vector Triple Product

$$ \vec{A} \times (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) = (\vec{A} \cdot \vec{C})\vec{B} - (\vec{A} \cdot \vec{B})\vec{C} $$

7. Vector Components and Unit Vectors

$$ \vec{A} = A_x\hat{i} + A_y\hat{j} + A_z\hat{k} $$
$$ \hat{A} = \vec{A}/|\vec{A}| $$

8. Vector Equations

$$ \vec{r} = \vec{a} + t\vec{b} $$
$$ (\vec{r} - \vec{a}) \cdot \vec{n} = 0 $$
$$ |\vec{r} - \vec{a}| = R $$

9. Applications in Physics

Work: \( W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} \)

Torque: \( \vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F} \)

Angular momentum: \( \vec{L} = \vec{r} \times \vec{p} \)

10. Vector Identities and Simplifications

$$ \nabla \cdot (\vec{A} \times \vec{B}) = \vec{B} \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{A}) - \vec{A} \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{B}) $$
$$ \nabla \times (\nabla \phi) = 0 $$
$$ \nabla \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{A}) = 0 $$
$$ \nabla \times (\nabla \times \vec{A}) = \nabla(\nabla \cdot \vec{A}) - \nabla^2 \vec{A} $$

11. Vector Differentiation

$$ \frac{d}{dt}(\vec{A} + \vec{B}) = \frac{d\vec{A}}{dt} + \frac{d\vec{B}}{dt} $$
$$ \frac{d}{dt}(a\vec{A}) = \frac{da}{dt} \vec{A} + a \frac{d\vec{A}}{dt} $$
$$ \frac{d}{dt}(\vec{A} \cdot \vec{B}) = \frac{d\vec{A}}{dt} \cdot \vec{B} + \vec{A} \cdot \frac{d\vec{B}}{dt} $$
$$ \frac{d}{dt}(\vec{A} \times \vec{B}) = \frac{d\vec{A}}{dt} \times \vec{B} + \vec{A} \times \frac{d\vec{B}}{dt} $$

12. Vector Integration

\( \int (\vec{A} + \vec{B}) dt = \int \vec{A} dt + \int \vec{B} dt \)

Line integrals: \( \int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} \)

Surface integrals: \( \int_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{S} \)

Volume integrals: \( \int_V \vec{F} dV \)

13. Orthogonal and Orthonormal Vectors

Orthogonal: \( \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} = 0 \)

Orthonormal: Orthogonal and unit length

Basis: \( \{\hat{i}, \hat{j}, \hat{k}\} \) standard orthonormal basis in 3D.

14. Gradient, Divergence, and Curl

Gradient: \( \nabla \phi \)

Divergence: \( \nabla \cdot \vec{F} \)

Curl: \( \nabla \times \vec{F} \)

15. Stokes’ Theorem

$$ \int_S (\nabla \times \vec{F}) \cdot d\vec{S} = \oint_{\partial S} \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} $$

16. Green’s Theorem

$$ \oint_C (P\,dx + Q\,dy) = \iint_D \left(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial P}{\partial y}\right) dA $$

17. Gauss’s Divergence Theorem

$$ \oint_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{S} = \iiint_V (\nabla \cdot \vec{F}) dV $$

18. Coordinate Systems

Polar Coordinates (2D)

$$ x = r\cos\theta,\quad y = r\sin\theta $$

Cylindrical Coordinates (3D)

$$ x = r\cos\theta,\quad y = r\sin\theta,\quad z = z $$

Spherical Coordinates (3D)

$$ x = r\sin\theta\cos\phi,\quad y = r\sin\theta\sin\phi,\quad z = r\cos\theta $$
