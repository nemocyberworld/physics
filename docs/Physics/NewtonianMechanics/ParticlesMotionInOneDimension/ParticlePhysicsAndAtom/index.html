Particles of Physics and the Structure of Atoms and Nuclei

1. Introduction

Everything in the universe is made of particles. From the air we breathe to the stars in the sky, all matter is composed of tiny building blocks. 2. Fundamental Particles

Physics breaks down matter into elementary particles. These are the smallest units that cannot be broken down further. There are two main families:

  • Fermions: These include quarks and leptons. They make up matter.
  • Bosons: These are force-carrying particles (like the photon).

2.1 Leptons

Leptons include electrons and neutrinos. Electrons are crucial for electricity and chemical bonds.

2.2 Quarks

Quarks combine to form protons and neutrons. There are six types, but the up and down quarks are the most common in ordinary matter.

Proton = 2 up quarks + 1 down quark
Neutron = 1 up quark + 2 down quarks

3. Atomic Structure

An atom consists of a nucleus (containing protons and neutrons) and a cloud of electrons orbiting it.

3.1 The Bohr Model

In the Bohr model, electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed paths called shells.

$$ E_n = - \frac{13.6\,\text{eV}}{n^2} \quad \text{(Energy of electron in the nth orbit)} $$

Where \( n \) is the principal quantum number.

3.2 Real-World Example: Chemistry

Electron arrangements determine how atoms bond and react. Water (H2O) forms because of hydrogen and oxygen atoms sharing electrons.

4. Nuclear Structure

The nucleus is made of protons and neutrons held together by the strong nuclear force.

4.1 Isotopes

Atoms with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons are called isotopes.

Example: Carbon-12 and Carbon-14 are both carbon but have different numbers of neutrons.

4.2 Radioactivity

Some isotopes are unstable and decay, emitting radiation.

$$ \text{Alpha decay: } ^{238}_{92}\text{U} \rightarrow ^{234}_{90}\text{Th} + ^{4}_{2}\text{He} $$

4.3 Applications

  • Medicine: PET scans use radioactive tracers.
  • Energy: Nuclear power plants use fission reactions.
  • Space: Radioactive decay powers some space probes.

5. Fun Facts

  • If the nucleus were the size of a golf ball, the atom would be the size of a football stadium!
  • 99.9% of an atom's mass is in the nucleus.
  • Stars shine because of nuclear fusion, where hydrogen atoms fuse into helium, releasing massive energy.

6. Summary

  • Everything is made of particles.
  • Atoms are made of a nucleus and electrons.
  • Protons and neutrons are made of quarks.
  • Nuclear physics explains radioactivity and fusion.
